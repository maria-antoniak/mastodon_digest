There is a radio bit that I found particularly thought provoking, I'm pretty sure it was the last act of this episode: https://www.thisamericanlife.org/165/americans-in-paris

In any case the TLDR: An American moves to Paris, is identified as and treated well as an American, independently of her skin color. She is very pleased and tells her friends back home that the French are not racist.

Then her French improved, to the point that she could be assumed to come from a (former) French colony, and received less respect in shops and so forth.

I haven't followed up enough to say whether French treatment of ex-colonials is "structural" in any meaningful way, but obviously it is persistent on some fronts. My takeaway is that racism takes different forms in different cultures.
